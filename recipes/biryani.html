Recipe For Chicken Biryani

Description

Chicken biryani is a fragrant rice dish made by layering marinated chicken with partially cooked basmati rice and aromatic spices. Start by marinating 500g chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt for at least 30 minutes. In a large pot, fry sliced onions in oil until golden, then add the marinated chicken and cook until it’s halfway done. Meanwhile, parboil 2 cups basmati rice with whole spices like bay leaves, cloves, and cardamom. In the same pot, layer the chicken and rice, sprinkle fried onions, mint, and coriander between layers, and drizzle with saffron milk.

Seal the pot with a tight lid or dough and cook ("dum") on low heat for about 25–30 minutes until the chicken is tender and rice is fully cooked. Let it rest for 10 minutes before gently mixing. Serve hot with raita or a simple salad for a complete meal bursting with flavor and aroma.

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken (bone-in preferred)
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2–3 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Whole spices (2 bay leaves, 4 cloves, 3 green cardamoms, 1 small cinnamon stick)
  • A handful of fresh mint leaves
  • A handful of fresh coriander leaves
  • A few strands of saffron soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Marinate chicken with yogurt, spices, and salt.
  2. Fry onions until golden brown.
  3. Cook marinated chicken until halfway done.
  4. Parboil basmati rice with whole spices.
  5. Layer chicken, rice, fried onions, mint, and coriander.
  6. Drizzle with saffron milk.
  7. Seal pot and cook on low heat ("dum") for 25–30 minutes.
  8. Rest, gently mix, and serve hot.
